## Hi, I'm Isaac

I co-founded Peanut Butter Sundays, a youth-run 501(c)(3) in LA, and I handle its operations and its tech. We started by handing PB&J sandwiches to unhoused neighbors and it grew into basketball tournaments, skate meetups, and a music festival that put housed and unhoused people in the same place. This fall I start at USC in Computational Neuroscience, focused on where the brain and AI overlap. I'm taking a year first to build.

Most of what I make is small, self-contained, and runs in a browser with no install. Three I'm proud of:

- **[pbs-tools](https://github.com/lllove514/pbs-tools)**. The two things I built for Peanut Butter Sundays: a map of LA County shaded by the 2025 homeless count, and Jelly, a guardrailed site assistant that runs on Claude with its key kept server-side.
- **[connectome-lab](https://github.com/lllove514/connectome-lab)**. The whole 302-neuron C. elegans nervous system in the browser. Click a neuron and watch a real leaky integrate-and-fire signal spread through the actual 2019 synapse data. A compare mode runs the same model on the real wiring and on a random rewiring of the same size, so you can see what the wiring itself is doing.
- **[hawaii-atlas](https://github.com/lllove514/hawaii-atlas)**. Four maps of Hawai'i (reef heat, lava flows, watersheds, rainfall) built from NOAA, USGS, and state data. Each one has a Python pipeline you can rerun from the raw sources.

Outside of code I'm into interpretability, computational neuroscience, and music production, and I read a lot.
